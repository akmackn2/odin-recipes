The Necessary Ingredients For The Cake:

  • 2 large Peeled Potatoes
  • Cup of Thawed Frozen Peas
  • 2 tablespoons of Vegetable Oil
  • 1/2 tspn of Cumin Seeds
  • 1 crushed Bay Leaf
  • 1 ibs of Ground Beef
  • 2 large finely chopped onions
  • 4 cloves Crushed Garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of Minched Fresh Ginger Root
  • 1.5 tspn of Salt
  • 1 tspn of Ground Cumin
  • 1 tspn of Ground Coriander
  • 1 tspn of Ground Turmeric
  • 1 tspn of Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tspn of Ground Cinnamon
  • 1/2 tspn of Ground Cardamom
  • 1/2 tspn of Ground Black Pepper
  • Quart of Cooking Oil to be used for deep frying
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh chopped Cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped Green Chile Peppers
  • 1 16oz. of Package Phyllo Dough

The Necessary Ingredients For The Frosting:

  • 3/4 cup of unsalted butter at room temperature
  • 5 1/3 cups Confectioners' Sugar
  • 1.5 cups of unsweetened Cocoa powder
  • 2/3 cups of Milk
  • 1 tspn of Vanilla extract

The Directions to Completion:

  1. Bring a medium saucepan of lightly salted water to a boil. Stir in potatoes and peas; cook until potatoes are tender but still firm for about 15 minutes. Drain, mash together, and set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf; stir until fragrant and seeds are browned. Add ground beef and onions; cook until beef is browned and crumbly and onions are soft for about 5 to 7 minutes.
  3. Stir in garlic and ginger. Season with salt, cumin, coriander, turmeric, chili powder, cinnamon, cardamom, and black pepper. Stir in the mashed potato mixture. Remove from heat and chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour, or until cool.
  4. Heat a quart of oil in a deep saucepan over medium heat.
  5. Mix cilantro and green chile peppers into potato and beef mixture. Place approximately 1 tablespoon of the mixture onto each phyllo sheet. Fold sheets into triangles, pressing edges together with moistened fingers.
  6. Lower samosas carefully into the hot oil in batches. Fry until golden brown, about 3 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain. Repeat with remaining samosas. Serve warm.
